Rainbows: The Science & Beauty

A rainbow is a stunning optical phenomenon that occurs when sunlight passes through raindrops, bending and splitting into its component colors — red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet. Water droplets act like tiny prisms, refracting and internally reflecting light to create a multicolored arc.

The appearance of a rainbow depends on the precise alignment of sunlight, raindrops, and the observer. Each color bends at a slightly different angle due to its wavelength, producing the familiar color bands.

Beyond physics, rainbows have long symbolized hope and beauty across cultures. Their brief yet vibrant presence makes them one of nature’s most magical sights.
